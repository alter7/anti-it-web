The Information Asymmetry

The global IT services market operates on a fundamental flaw: the client rarely understands what they are buying. This asymmetry allows vendors to inflate hours, over-engineer architectures, and lock clients into proprietary ecosystems. The industry thrives on client ignorance.

The 45% Rule & Black Swans

This is not a conspiracy; it is a documented economic reality. A landmark study of over 5,400 IT projects conducted by McKinsey & Company in collaboration with the University of Oxford revealed devastating statistics. On average, large IT projects run 45% over budget and 7% over time. Worse, 17% of these projects turn into "black swans"—catastrophic failures with budget overruns exceeding 200%, threatening the financial stability of the companies that commissioned them.

The $2.41 Trillion Technical Debt

The cost of poor software quality and over-engineered bloat is staggering. According to the Consortium for Information & Software Quality (CISQ) 2022 Report, poor software quality cost the U.S. economy at least $2.41 trillion. Of that, $1.52 trillion is accumulated technical debt—the direct result of vendors choosing complex, high-maintenance stacks over practical, context-appropriate solutions.
