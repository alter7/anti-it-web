CASE LAW REGISTRY

Verified legal precedents where independent forensic audits exposed vendor fraud, personnel swapping, and catastrophic over-engineering.

Case ID / Status Conflict Details Audit Verdict Outcome
#L-2201Decision Final MillerCoors vs. HCL Technologies
A $100M lawsuit regarding a botched SAP implementation. The client alleged project mismanagement and lack of professional standards.
Verdict: Personnel SwappingAudit revealed that the vendor billed senior expert rates while actually deploying junior, unqualified staff. Undisclosed settlement; claims dismissed after remediation. Source: UpperEdge
#L-0842Settled Hertz vs. Accenture
Failure of a $32M digital transformation. The vendor delivered a non-responsive website that ignored agreed-upon requirements. 		Verdict: Professional NegligenceForensic code review proved the product was not "professional quality" and missed critical functional milestones. Undisclosed settlement fund; contract terminated. Source: Reuters
#L-0012Decision Final Post Office (UK) vs. Subpostmasters
The "Horizon" system created false shortfalls in thousands of branch accounts, leading to wrongful prosecutions. 		Verdict: Hidden System DefectsIndependent auditors (Second Sight) exposed that the system was riddled with bugs the vendor failed to disclose for 15 years. £1B+ compensation; 100+ criminal convictions overturned. Source: BBC News
#L-3910Decision Final Revlon vs. SAP / Fujitsu
An ERP launch disaster that caused a $64M drop in stock value and paralyzed the supply chain for months. 		Verdict: Material WeaknessThe audit confirmed that critical internal controls were bypassed during the high-risk implementation phase. Class-action settlement paid to shareholders for undisclosed IT risks. Source: Bloomberg
#L-0055Settled NHS vs. Fujitsu / CSC
The £12.7B "National Programme for IT" — often cited as the largest IT failure in public sector history. 		Verdict: Over-Specification WasteSystems were designed with massive unnecessary complexity, making them untestable and unusable. £700M payout to the supplier after a botched contract termination. Source: Panorama Consulting
